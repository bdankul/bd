DID YOU KNOW?

When you see color, you're actually just seeing light!

ISAAC NEWTON

In 1665, the young scientist Isaac Newton held a glass prism to the sunlight and discovered that white light is made up of all the colors that we can see. Color is only possible with light.

COLOR AND LIGHT

When light passes through the solid glass it becomes distorted and comes out the other side a different wavelength.

Each color our human eyes can see is a different wavelength on the visible spectrum.

COLOR SPECTRUM

Newton organized the colors into the visible color spectrum and from there, color theory and the color wheel were developed.

Mixing combinations of primary colors can get you any color of the rainbow!

  • red yellow blue

Secondary colors are made by mixing the primary colors.

  • blue + yellow = green
  • red + blue = violet
  • yellow + red = orange

Tertiary colors are made by mixing the new neighboring colors on the color wheel. We get colors like magenta and red orange
